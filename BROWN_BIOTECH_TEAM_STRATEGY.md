# Brown Biotech 10-Person Team Strategy

## Strategy summary
The 10-person team should not act like a committee.
It should act like a small operating system that converts interest into revenue and repeatable proof.

## Strategic choice
**Primary motion:** Paid Brief → peptide-service

**Secondary motions:**
- biostatx for analysis/reporting
- genox-site for discovery framing/partner scoping
- business-pipeline for intake routing and automation

## Team thesis
If the team can do three things reliably, Brown Biotech grows:
1. turn inbound interest into a brief
2. turn briefs into routed delivery
3. turn delivery into proof that helps the next brief close faster

## 10-person structure
- Strategy / approval
- Intake
- Triage
- Routing
- Peptide service
- Biostatx
- Genox-site
- Business pipeline
- Proof / publications
- QA / launch checks

## What to do next
### 1) Lock the message
Use the same words everywhere:
- Request a Brief
- one brief
- one owner
- one next action
- human approval for high-stakes actions

### 2) Tighten the offer ladder
- Trial / sample scope review
- Paid brief
- Project
- Retainer

### 3) Run the weekly loop
Every week answer:
- What came in?
- What was routed?
- What was delivered?
- What was blocked?
- What should be reused?

### 4) Build proof in public
Publish small, credible items that support the primary lane.

### 5) Keep the HQ visible
The Notion HQ should show:
- live status
- active lanes
- blockers
- approvals
- next actions

## 30-day team objective
By day 30, the team should have:
- one stable intake flow
- one stable paid-brief path
- one proof/publication stream
- one partner funnel
- one weekly review cadence

## Decision rule
If a task does not improve intake, routing, delivery, proof, or review, do not add it.
