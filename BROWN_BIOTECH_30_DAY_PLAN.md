# Brown Biotech 30-Day Plan

## Objective
Turn the current Brown Biotech positioning into a repeatable operating motion:
**Paid Brief → peptide-service → routed delivery**

## Operating rules
- One brief
- One owner
- One next action
- Human approval for high-stakes actions
- Keep the website, Notion HQ, and intake flow aligned

## Success criteria by day 30
- One clear primary motion in every surface
- One stable intake flow that routes requests cleanly
- One visible proof layer: publications, case notes, or research signals
- One partner funnel that turns signal into a routed brief
- One weekly review rhythm that produces decisions, not discussion only

## Week 1 — Lock the system
**Goal:** remove ambiguity.

Deliverables:
- Confirm the primary lane: `peptide-service`
- Confirm supporting lanes: `biostatx`, `genox-site`, `business-pipeline`
- Confirm approval gates for money, legal, contracts, deployment, public claims, and medical claims
- Confirm the HQ structure in Notion
- Confirm the intake fields and routing rules

Outputs:
- HQ snapshot
- routing map
- approval checklist
- one-page offer hierarchy

## Week 2 — Strengthen the intake path
**Goal:** make it easy to submit, route, and review.

Deliverables:
- Keep `Request a Brief` as the primary CTA
- Tighten the inquiry flow
- Ensure the receipt shows route, owner, and next action
- Add or refine the sample brief if needed
- Create a clean disqualify / no-fit path

Outputs:
- intake template
- routing receipt
- fit / no-fit decision rules
- updated service copy

## Week 3 — Build proof and trust
**Goal:** make the business credible at a glance.

Deliverables:
- Publish or draft 3–5 short research/publication items
- Add case-note style proof to the HQ
- Keep the partner path visible but not noisy
- Make the proof layer readable in under 30 seconds

Outputs:
- publication list
- proof cards
- partner-ready summary
- updated homepage copy if needed

## Week 4 — Make it repeatable
**Goal:** turn the motion into a loop.

Deliverables:
- Run a weekly review with wins, blockers, risks, and next actions
- Identify what should become a reusable template
- Capture any repeated request patterns
- Decide the next lane investment based on actual demand

Outputs:
- weekly review note
- decision log
- reusable templates
- next 30-day priorities

## Daily operating loop
1. Capture inbound request
2. Triage fit and urgency
3. Route to lane and owner
4. Draft or update the brief
5. Record human approval if needed
6. Deliver a shareable receipt
7. Add the outcome to weekly review

## Risks to watch
- Broad offer scope before the primary lane is repeatable
- Free-consulting behavior disguised as “helpfulness”
- Proof content that is too long or too vague
- A partner funnel without a real next action
- High-stakes decisions moving without review

## Immediate next actions
1. Keep `peptide-service` first everywhere.
2. Align HQ sections with the live website copy.
3. Publish the first short proof/publication item.
4. Keep the intake receipt visible and actionable.
5. Review weekly and remove friction.
6. Use the new daily signal workflow for live, article-level verification.
