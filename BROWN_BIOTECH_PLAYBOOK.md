# Brown Biotech Playbook

This is the internal operating playbook for Brown Biotech.
Use it when a request arrives, a lane needs to be chosen, or a brief needs to become a deliverable.

## Operating principle
- One brief
- One owner
- One next action
- Human approval for high-stakes actions

## Flow
1. **Intake** — capture the request in a structured form
2. **Triage** — score fit, urgency, and approval risk
3. **Route** — assign lane and owner
4. **Brief** — draft the paid brief or sample brief
5. **Spec** — convert the brief into a short implementation spec when needed
6. **Task** — break the spec into approved tasks
7. **Deliver** — create a shareable receipt or output
8. **Review** — capture learnings and update the hub

## Internal commands
Think of these as the team’s shorthand:
- **/skills** — open the reusable skill pack
- **/playbook** — open this document
- **/hub** — open the Notion operating hub
- **/brief** — create the paid brief
- **/route** — decide the lane and owner
- **/review** — run the weekly review loop

## Service routing
### peptide-service
Use when the request is about peptide work, quote path, or a consultation.

### biostatx
Use when the request needs dataset review, analysis planning, or reporting.

### genox-site
Use when the request is about discovery framing or partner scoping.

### business-pipeline
Use when the request is about operating systems, intake routing, or service automation.

## What to keep out
- Unscoped free consulting
- Open-ended AI hype
- High-stakes actions without review
- Extra lanes that do not reduce friction

## Weekly control loop
- What came in?
- What was routed?
- What was delivered?
- What blocked?
- What should become a reusable template?

## Success condition
The playbook is working when the same language appears in:
- the website
- the Notion hub
- the intake form
- the receipt
- the weekly review
