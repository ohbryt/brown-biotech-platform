# OpenMed vs Brown Biotech

## One-line comparison
- **OpenMed:** controlled clinical workflow runtime
- **Brown Biotech:** decision-ready business routing system

## What OpenMed does well
- Makes modes explicit
- Shows runtime, trace, and artifacts
- Emphasizes human review
- Uses trust language that is short and sharp
- Onboards through a very clear sequence

## What Brown Biotech does well
- Has a real paid-brief motion
- Already routes by lane / owner / approval gate
- Connects the front door to receipts and next actions
- Can turn requests into business assets, not just outputs

## Best ideas to borrow
1. **Mode labels**
   - Brief
   - Discovery
   - Delivery
   - Review
   - Track

2. **Trust language**
   - Inspectable workflow
   - Human review required
   - Privacy-aware execution
   - Decision-ready output
   - No blind automation

3. **Onboarding sequence**
   - Request a brief
   - Route it
   - Review it
   - Deliver it
   - Track it

4. **Visible runtime evidence**
   - current route
   - owner
   - approval gate
   - next action
   - receipt

## Brown Biotech rule
Use OpenMed-style clarity, but keep Brown Biotech’s business structure visible.

### Translation
- OpenMed-style clarity → make the system easy to trust
- Brown Biotech structure → make the system easy to buy

## Immediate operating rule
**Request a Paid Brief → triage → route → owner → receipt → next action**

## Immediate product rule
If a page feels vague, replace abstract claims with:
- the lane name
- the owner
- the approval rule
- the next step
- the receipt
