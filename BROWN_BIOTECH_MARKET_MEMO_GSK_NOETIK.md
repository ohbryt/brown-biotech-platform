# Brown Biotech Market Memo: GSK × Noetik

## Thesis
The GSK × Noetik deal signals a new biotech AI monetization category:

**Foundation Model License Subscription**
- trained biological foundation models are licensed as enterprise assets
- revenue comes from upfront fees, milestones, and recurring subscription value
- the model itself becomes a product, not just an internal capability

## Why it matters
This matters because it extends the biotech AI deal stack beyond:
- drug deals
- infrastructure deals
- enterprise OS deals
- co-innovation lab deals
- end-to-end LLM deals

Now a company can monetize:
1. the dataset
2. the trained model
3. the recurring license
4. the service layer around deployment and adoption

## What the GSK × Noetik pattern suggests
- Big pharma is willing to buy specialized biological foundation models for narrow, high-value indications.
- Non-exclusive licensing can preserve upside while scaling recurring revenue.
- Annual subscription structure makes the model feel like enterprise software, not a one-off research collaboration.
- The commercial value is not only in predictions, but in the speed of moving from biology data to decision support.

## Brown Biotech implication
Brown Biotech should treat this as a signal to build durable assets in three layers:
1. **Paid brief layer** — decision-ready research and intake routing
2. **Workflow layer** — repeatable execution, proof, and review loops
3. **Foundation asset layer** — structured knowledge, templates, models, and reusable operating artifacts

## Strategic takeaway
Brown Biotech does not need to become a general AI platform.
It should become a **decision engine with reusable operating assets** that can later support recurring products, subscriptions, and specialized knowledge delivery.

## Immediate action
Add this category to the internal business-model matrix and use it as a reference when evaluating future biotech AI partnerships.

## One-line framing
**From data to model to recurring license: the new biotech AI revenue path.**
