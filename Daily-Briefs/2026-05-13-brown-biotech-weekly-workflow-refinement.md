# Brown Biotech Weekly Workflow-Refinement Memo

**Date:** 2026-05-13
**Workflow:** Brown Biotech Weekly Review

## Strongest recurring signals
1. **FDA releases remain the highest-value regulatory signal** for spotting outsourcing demand shifts and compliance tailwinds.
2. **Biotech trade press + company newsrooms** are the best early indicators of partnerships, funding, and manufacturing scale-up.
3. **AI-native discovery and peptide/CDMO updates** consistently map to Brown’s near-term opportunities: rapid-turn custom peptides, assay-support peptides, and CMC/ops support.

## Weakest / noisiest parts
1. Nature Biotechnology was still not reliably accessible in this environment, so it remains a best-effort source rather than a guaranteed daily input.
2. The workflow now has live article examples, but it still needs a stronger automated collection layer so the memo is not manually assembled.
3. The relevance score is useful, but we should keep tightening what counts as true Brown-relevant signal versus generic biotech news.

## Prompt / source improvement
- Use **live article-level verification** with a simple relevance score for: **peptide demand**, **licensing/partnerships**, **regulatory/funding shift**.
- Include **coverage window, headline, source, and why it matters** so the memo is based on evidence, not source-family inference.
- Keep Nature Biotechnology as a secondary source unless the access path is stable.

## Business action
- Stand up a **weekly alert/ranking workflow** that surfaces only the top Brown-relevant items and converts them into an outreach list for **AI-drug platform teams, peptide/CDMO targets, and fast-moving discovery groups**.

## Decision
**Refine** — the source mix is right and live verification is now partially working, but the workflow still needs a more reliable automated collection layer before it becomes fully decision-grade.

## Bottom line
The current brief is a good **signal map**, and now a partial **decision engine**. Keep the source families, tighten the intake, and keep moving toward article-level prioritization.
